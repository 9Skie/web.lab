We usually hear something like 'frontend designer', 'UI UX', but what are these at all?

- UI - User Interface: this is how people feel how 'fancy' your website is.

- UX - User Experience: this is how people feel when they actually navigate around the website.

![[Screenshot 2026-06-30 at 4.30.17 PM.png]]

We usually want UI to follow a 'theme/guideline', whether that be fonts, color templates, shapes, etc, you want them to be in unison.

## UI

This is probably not the class for 'design' so to speak... frontend design is it's whole own gimmick on it's own, and there's barely any science to this, it's just 'taste'.

For example, MIT's own frontend changed a lot over time, again it's just 'taste'.

![[Screenshot 2026-06-30 at 9.44.32 PM.png|500]]

The UI of individual icons & elements also change over time, like this of the trash can Icon.

There's also many websites with existing UI templates & components, you can use them as inspiration and (steal them) to make your own.

## UX

This is the opposite of UI, somewhat, the whole point is the experience of the user navigating through the website, is information placed a manner such that the user knows how to work their way around things and isn't overloaded?
- Again... this is like fiddling around by user experience, there's no science to this

But we can have a comparison, this is facebook's old login page, you have a bunch of things you can fill out, name, surname, email or phone... it's overwhelming.

![[Screenshot 2026-06-30 at 10.01.39 PM.png|500]]

Compare that to the new facebook UI... clean for the user experience, they know what to do instantly

![[Screenshot 2026-06-30 at 10.02.24 PM.png|500]]
